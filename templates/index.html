Finding a physician's prescribing DNA

Pharmaceutical companies assist in physician education by using field medical and sales teams to engage with physicians and provide the latest clinical information about their product. However, with greater than 14,000 hematologist and oncologists in the U.S., sales and marketing executives are tasked with determining how to best utilize their sales and marketing resources quickly and effectively.

With publicly-available data, Med Mappr profiles a physician's prescribing habits and analyzes the drug marketplace to identify opportunities for physician engagement. Med mappr seeks to answer some of these questions:

  • How readily does a physician adopt new drugs in place of more established ones?
  • Determine how demographics influence prescribing behavior

Analysis of Oncology Prescribing Behavior

This project focuses on predicting top-prescribing physicians for oncology drugs. Using Imbruvica, an oral inhibitor of Bruton's tyrosine kinase for treatment of mantel cell lymphoma and chronic lymphocytic leukemia, I analyzed Medicare claims data to build a gradient boosted classifier model that predicts whether a physician will be a top prescriber in the following year.

Targeting Underserved Practices

Providers located in rural areas of the U.S. may not have as much access to medical education resources or peer networks. Access to medical educational personnel has been shown to influence early adoption of new therapies.
